Workscape pioneers a new perspective on workspaces, challenging stereotypes by placing a work desk in unconventional areas. Through interactive elements, it sheds light on the diverse thinking styles of neurodivergent individuals, advocating for inclusive and flexible work environments beyond conventional offices. The project aims to foster understanding, encouraging a shift towards more accommodating and diverse workspaces.
Background

As workplaces evolve, there's a growing recognition of the need for inclusive environments. However, conventional office setups often fail to accommodate the diverse needs of neurodivergent individuals. Workscape emerges amidst this backdrop, aiming to challenge traditional workspace norms and advocate for more inclusive settings.

Target

Workscape is designed for organizations seeking to foster inclusive work environments. Our audience includes employers, HR professionals, workspace designers, and advocates for diversity.

Directed Storytelling

Utilizing the directed storytelling research method, we gathered crucial insights from industry experts and neurodivergent individuals regarding workplace experiences, informing the development of Workscape.

Findings

Our research revealed several key findings highlighting the importance of disability inclusion in the workplace and its impact on future work dynamics:

  • Disability Inclusion: Hiring individuals with disabilities enhances workforce stability and fosters innovation, benefiting companies and stakeholders.
  • Importance of Neurodiversity: Neurodivergent individuals, representing diverse talents, contribute uniquely to the workforce, necessitating greater inclusivity.
  • Challenges Faced by Neurodivergent Individuals: Insights from neurodivergent individuals reveal the obstacles they encounter in understanding tasks and accessing adequate support, underscoring the importance of accommodation and understanding.
  • Perspectives from Recruiters: Many companies lack specific policies addressing neurodiversity, indicating a need for inclusive strategies and increased awareness to support neurodivergent employees effectively.

Why is it important to shift societal perceptions towards neurodiversity and embrace diverse thinking styles in the workplace?

Shifting perceptions to embrace neurodiversity in the workplace is crucial for fostering diversity and inclusivity. By valuing the unique perspectives of neurodivergent individuals, organizations unlock untapped talent potential, driving innovation and creating a positive workplace culture. Embracing neurodiversity also broadens market appeal, aligning businesses with diverse customer needs. Ultimately, it's about leveraging the full spectrum of human potential for collective benefit.

What do we want to achieve?

Promote a change in mindset regarding neurodiversity, emphasizing the value of diverse thinking styles.

How do we want to achieve it?

  • By educating society about the diversity of thinking and functioning among individuals.
  • By highlighting the benefits of diverse thinking in problem-solving and creativity.
  • By advocating for transparent discussions about the advantages and challenges faced by neurodivergent individuals.
  • By expanding the concept of diversity beyond physical attributes to include cognitive diversity.

Our Approach

A trans-media experience that educates people about the challenges that aims to foster awareness and understanding of invisible disabilities in the workplace.

Transmedia Experiece

Using transmedia extends the story across platforms, enhancing engagement and accessibility. It provides a consistent, interactive, and innovative experience, promoting broader reach and inclusivity. This approach, inspired by successful franchises like Harry Potter, creates a memorable experience for diverse audiences.

What is neurodiversity?

Neurodiversity refers to the wide range of differences in how individuals' brains function. It commonly encompasses conditions such as autism spectrum disorder (ASD), ADHD, and learning disabilities, which affect neurological or developmental functioning (Nicole Baumer & Julia Frueh, 2021).
