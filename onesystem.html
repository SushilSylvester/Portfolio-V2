One System

Unifying Design at Mercedes Benz R&D India
At Mercedes-Benz R&D India (MBRDI), I saw an opportunity to unify UI patterns across internal tools. Fragmented design choices and inconsistent components were affecting usability and slowing development. As part of the central UX team, I led the effort to create a scalable, consistent design system tailored for our internal ecosystem.

Within our internal applications team, I created a Figma-based component library to bring consistency and efficiency to the design process. Built in collaboration with designers and developers, it reduced duplication of effort and laid the foundation for future design improvements.
Background

Designers were recreating components from scratch, using inconsistent styles and screenshots. This lack of standardization led to misaligned visuals, inefficient workflows, and inconsistent user experiences across the product. Developers also lacked clear design specs, slowing down implementation.

Target

Create a unified, Figma-based component library that standardizes design, improves collaboration, reduces rework, and enables designers and developers to build consistent, high-quality screens more efficiently.

Research & Discovery

Identifying inconsistencies and aligning teams through audits, interviews, and industry benchmarking.

1. Auditing the Existing System

I conducted a thorough audit of our application’s UI and compared it against best practices from Material Design and Carbon Design System. This helped me identify layout misalignments, inconsistent typography, hardcoded color usage, and missing design standards. I also reviewed our developer Storybook to uncover gaps between design and implementation—insights that directly informed the foundation of the new component library.

Key issues found
  • Layout inconsistencies
  • Typography mismatches
  • Color variations due to hardcoded values
  • Lack of design–dev standardization

What happens when every designer builds with a different set of rules?

Without shared components or guidelines, designers were recreating elements from scratch, leading to inconsistent visuals, duplicated effort, and confusion across teams. Layouts, typography, and color choices varied widely—even for similar screens—making the product feel disjointed and unpolished. Developers struggled with unclear specs, and collaboration slowed down due to the lack of a single source of truth. This fragmentation made it harder to scale design and reduced overall efficiency.

What do we want to achieve?

To create a unified, scalable, and reusable design system that brings visual consistency, reduces duplicated work, and improves collaboration between designers and developers.

How do we want to achieve it?

  • Audit existing UI to identify inconsistencies and repetitive patterns
  • Study established systems like Material and Carbon to understand structure and scalability
  • Gather feedback from designers and developers on workflow gaps and pain points
  • Build a Figma-based component library with auto-layout, constraints, and design tokens
  • Document each component with usage rules, behaviors, and accessibility notes
  • Align with developers by leveraging Figma’s Dev Mode for smoother handoffs and fewer iterations

Final Outcome

The design system foundation initiative led to clear, measurable improvements in collaboration, consistency, and efficiency across product teams. By aligning designers and developers through shared components and guidelines, we reduced rework, streamlined the design-to-dev handoff, and established a scalable base for future growth.

Design & Dev Alignment

The shared component library streamlined collaboration between designers and developers. With standardized tokens, naming conventions, and reusable building blocks, handoff friction was significantly reduced. Both teams began speaking the same language — saving time and avoiding rework.

Time Saved Across Teams

Teams reported measurable gains in speed and efficiency. Designers spent less time recreating patterns, and developers could implement features faster using tested, documented components. This translated to a faster time-to-launch across multiple projects.

Design Consistency

The design system unified previously scattered styles into a cohesive and professional visual language across all product areas. This consistency not only improved the user experience but also strengthened the overall brand identity, making interactions feel more seamless and reliable.
