PraiseLab revolutionizes workplace culture by introducing a novel platform for employee appreciation. It reimagines traditional methods by empowering individuals to express gratitude through handwritten notes, seamlessly integrated into daily office routines. By fostering a culture of recognition and connection, PraiseLab advocates for inclusive and supportive work environments. Through its interactive elements and digital infrastructure, the project aims to enhance employee morale, strengthen professional relationships, and promote a culture of appreciation that transcends conventional workplace norms.
Background

In today's evolving workplaces, breaking down silos and fostering collaboration among departments is paramount. However, traditional organizational structures often result in isolated departments and fragmented communication, impeding cross-functional collaboration and stifling innovation. PraiseLab challenges this status quo by offering a unique solution.

PraiseLab disrupts conventional norms by providing a platform that encourages appreciation and recognition across departments. By fostering a culture of openness, collaboration, and shared success, PraiseLab transforms organizational dynamics and enhances productivity.

Target

PraiseLab is designed for organizations dedicated to cultivating positive work cultures and boosting employee engagement. Our target audience includes HR professionals, organizational leaders, workplace designers, and advocates for workplace diversity and inclusion.

Interaction Experimentation

We experimented with innovative methods to foster interaction between silos. These included:

  • Airdrop Requests: Airdropping requests for cute images to random individuals.
  • Poster for Favorite Songs: Placing posters in corridors, inviting people to write down their favorite songs.
  • Interactive Game: Organizing interactive games with neighboring classes, using prompts for participation.

Findings

  • Airdrop Requests: Low Engagement - people dont want to interact with strangers.
  • Poster for Favorite Songs: High Engagement - people dont have restrictions on sharing non personal information.
  • Interactive Game: Limited Engagement - People didnt want to engage with other teams when they didnt have any incentive to do so.
  • The exploration of different interaction strategies revealed varying levels of success, highlighting the importance of selecting methods that align closely with audience preferences and behaviors.
  • These findings offer valuable insights into effective engagement strategies, informing future endeavors aimed at fostering interaction among individuals within diverse contexts.

Interviews with Council Member

The team conducted structured interviews with employees from the Southwark council to understand work culture, communication dynamics, and collaboration experiences. Questions were carefully crafted, drawing from prior research and team insights. Teams collaborated to refine questions and establish a cohesive flow.

Findings

  • Insights into Work Culture: Gained valuable insights into council's work culture, including communication norms and collaboration practices.
  • Identification of Communication Issues: Revealed challenges like siloed communication and barriers to interdepartmental collaboration.
  • Understanding Collaboration Experiences: Highlighted positive instances and areas for improvement in collaboration.
  • Preparation for Follow-up Questions:Facilitated identification of follow-up questions based on initial responses.
  • Informed Development of Interview Questions: Findings informed development of relevant questions, aligning with research objectives.

Research on Office Spaces and Interactions

Selected concepts were tested at the Barbican Center, including metaphors and games, refining our understanding of effective interactions. Additionally, we researched office spaces and their role in communication, studying design's impact on collaboration.

Findings

  • Role of Spaces in Interpersonal Interactions: Spaces significantly shape interpersonal interactions within office environments.
  • Influence of Furniture Design: Furniture arrangement and design exert considerable influence on interactions.
  • Examination of Major Companies' Office Designs: Study of major companies' office designs provided insights into effective strategies for fostering collaboration and communication.

How can organizations effectively break down silos in the workplace to promote collaboration and innovation among employees?

Breaking down workplace silos requires creating a culture where people across departments collaborate openly. This means encouraging teamwork, transparent communication, and sharing ideas. By promoting interaction and collaboration, organizations can foster a more united and innovative workplace.

What do we want to achieve?

Our goal is to cultivate a culture of appreciation and connection within the workplace, breaking down silos between different departments and fostering mutual recognition of employees' contributions.

How do we want to achieve it?

  • Focusing on fostering appreciation: By creating immersive experiences that tangibly represent the value of appreciation in the workplace.
  • Incorporating gamification: Utilizing arcade-inspired displays and physical badges to gamify the process of showing appreciation.
  • Implementing a Company Integration Mentorship Program: Establishing a structured program to facilitate cross-departmental mentorship and collaboration.
  • Facilitating transparent communication: Broadcasting daily company-wide appreciation posts and showcasing them on a large screen in the lobby.
  • Encouraging inter-team appreciation: Promoting dialogue and recognition between teams through physical letter posts and interactive displays.

How might we

create an interactive solution that fosters cross-departmental collaboration and breaks down organizational silos to enhance workplace productivity and cohesion?

Our Approach

We focus on breaking down departmental barriers and enhancing productivity by encouraging collaboration between employees across different teams. Through innovative design and interactive initiatives, we aim to create an environment where employees feel empowered to connect, share ideas, and work together effectively.

Cross-Functional Integration

Our design approach centers on promoting cross-departmental collaboration to improve workplace dynamics and productivity. By fostering open communication and encouraging teamwork, we aim to break down traditional silos and create a culture of inclusivity and collaboration within the organization.

Why is it important to shift societal perceptions towards neurodiversity and embrace diverse thinking styles in the workplace?

Shifting perceptions to embrace neurodiversity in the workplace is crucial for fostering diversity and inclusivity. By valuing the unique perspectives of neurodivergent individuals, organizations unlock untapped talent potential, driving innovation and creating a positive workplace culture. Embracing neurodiversity also broadens market appeal, aligning businesses with diverse customer needs. Ultimately, it's about leveraging the full spectrum of human potential for collective benefit.

What do we want to achieve?

Promote a change in mindset regarding neurodiversity, emphasizing the value of diverse thinking styles.

How do we want to achieve it?

  • By educating society about the diversity of thinking and functioning among individuals.
  • By highlighting the benefits of diverse thinking in problem-solving and creativity.
  • By advocating for transparent discussions about the advantages and challenges faced by neurodivergent individuals.
  • By expanding the concept of diversity beyond physical attributes to include cognitive diversity.

How might we

develop an interactive platform that challenges conventional workspaces and raises awareness of invisible disabilities in the workplace, within public settings?

Our Approach

A trans-media experience that educates people about the challenges that aims to foster awareness and understanding of invisible disabilities in the workplace.

Transmedia Experiece

Using transmedia extends the story across platforms, enhancing engagement and accessibility. It provides a consistent, interactive, and innovative experience, promoting broader reach and inclusivity. This approach, inspired by successful franchises like Harry Potter, creates a memorable experience for diverse audiences.

Introducing

Workscape

A transmedia storytelling experience promoting awareness of invisible disabilities.

What we made

We crafted a unique work desk positioned in unconventional settings to champion inclusive workspaces, particularly for individuals with invisible disabilities. Adorned with physical elements symbolizing neurodiversity data, the desk featured integrated sensors activating an interactive app upon detecting passersby. This transmedia experience engaged users through interactive tasks, aiming to foster awareness and understanding of invisible disabilities. Upon completion, users were seamlessly directed to curated information resources and a project video, providing deeper insights and encouraging empathy towards the cause.

Elements of our trans media experience

1. Interactive Data Displays on the Table

We had various props on the table, each communicating different statistics and facts about neurodiversity in the workplace.

2. Website

Here is the wireframe of the website we created for the project. It includes all the information gathered from our research.

3. Interactive desktop application

We created an application using Python and Arduino. This app is activated when someone walks past the table. It plays a loud sound to capture the passerby's attention and displays tasks on the screen for them to perform on the table. As they complete these tasks, they learn more about neurodiversity in the workplace.

4. Social media

To attract attention on social media, we created surreal images of work desks located in different corners of the world using Blender.
