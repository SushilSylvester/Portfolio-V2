After Hours reimagines the nocturnal world of London's hotel industry, spotlighting the challenges faced by night shift workers. Through immersive experiences and tangible artefacts, the project seeks to raise awareness and advocate for better conditions for these essential workers. By blending narrative storytelling with critical design, After Hours invites viewers to engage with the realities of night shift work and envision a more supportive future for those working in unconventional hours.
Background

After Hours is inspired by the lives of night shift workers in the hotel industry. It seeks to shed light on their challenges and experiences, aiming to create awareness and advocate for better support and understanding for these essential workers.

Target

Our target audience encompasses the general public, policymakers, hotel management, and labor rights organizations. Through our project, we aim to raise awareness and advocate for better support for night shift workers in the hotel industry.

Research

We utilized several research methods to thoroughly understand the environment and the lives of hotel night shift workers in London.

1. Directed story-telling

Directed storytelling involved collecting personal stories from night shift workers in the hotel industry. These narratives shed light on the challenges they face daily, including isolation, irregular sleep patterns, and dietary issues. Through these stories, we gained valuable insights into the experiences and needs of night shift workers.

Findings

Through Directed Storytelling, we gained insights into the challenges faced by night shift workers in hotels. They highlighted concerns about their health, especially sleep patterns, alongside the positives like flexibility and pay. However, the interviews underscored the need for better support and understanding.

2. Love and break-up letter

In this exercise, night shift workers in the hotel industry wrote love and break-up letters to their jobs. These letters provided a unique opportunity to delve into the emotional connection and challenges experienced by workers during their night shifts. By personifying their relationship with work, we gained nuanced insights into their feelings and experiences, offering a deeper understanding of their perspective.

Findings

The Love/Breakup Letters revealed a mix of emotions from night shift workers. They appreciated financial incentives and free time but expressed concerns about health and personal life tolls. Overall, the letters emphasized the complex relationship between workers and their profession, urging solutions for improved conditions.

3. Psychogeography walk

The psychogeography walk involved exploring London's nocturnal landscape to understand the environment in which night shift workers operate. By immersing ourselves in the city's after-dark rhythm, we gained a deeper appreciation for the challenges and opportunities present in the nighttime economy. This experiential research method provided valuable context for our project.

Findings

Our Psychogeography walk in Leicester Square provided firsthand insights into the nighttime environment. Divided into groups, we documented our observations through audio recordings and photos. Key findings included the irregular work schedules and disrupted sleep patterns of night shift workers, along with safety concerns and the reliance on artificial light. The experience underscored the need for better-prepared interview questions to capture relevant insights effectively.

4. AEIOU

The AEIOU framework allowed us to analyze the activities, environments, interactions, objects, and users involved in the night shift work experience. Through this method, we identified key pain points and opportunities for improvement. By systematically examining each element, we developed a holistic understanding of the night shift worker's journey and the factors that influence their work environment.

Findings

During our AEIOU analysis, we observed various aspects of London's nightlife, shedding light on the nighttime atmosphere. Safety concerns emerged, particularly for female group members due to increased intoxication levels and a perceived lack of police presence. Additionally, observations highlighted the prevalence of soft lighting, bustling crowds, and diverse demographics.

Why is it crucial to raise awareness about the challenges faced by night shift workers in the hotel industry?

Night shift workers in the hotel industry face a myriad of challenges, including disrupted sleep patterns, safety concerns, irregular work schedules, reliance on artificial light, and the risk of long-term health effects. These challenges not only impact their well-being but also affect their personal lives. Moreover, night shift workers play a crucial role in sustaining the nighttime economy, making it imperative to address their concerns and ensure their well-being.

What do we want to achieve?

Raise awareness about the challenges faced by night shift workers in the hotel industry.

How do we want to achieve it?

  • By creating immersive experiences which serve as tangible representations of the challenges and experiences of night shift workers.
  • Through these interactive installations, we aim to evoke empathy and understanding among the general public and policymakers regarding the realities faced by night shift workers.
  • By leveraging innovative design and storytelling techniques, we seek to spark meaningful conversations and inspire action towards improving the working conditions and support systems for night shift workers in the hotel industry.
  • By employing critical design principles, we challenge preconceptions and provoke thought about the nighttime economy and the role of night shift workers.

Our Approach

We use a diverse strategy, combining experiences, storytelling, and engagement, to advocate for night shift hotel workers. Our aim is to highlight their challenges and spark significant changes in workplace policies and societal views.

Critical design

Our project uses critical design to challenge norms and reflect on societal issues, particularly treatment of night shift workers. With immersive experiences like the Sleep Serms and Conversation Lamp, we aim to provoke thought, spark conversations, and advocate for tangible change in support of these workers.

PROTOTYPING

Our prototyping phase transformed abstract concepts into tangible artefacts, enabling us to convey the challenges of night shift work effectively. Through iterative design and testing, we refined each prototype to align with our project goals. Below, we present the evolution of our prototypes and the testing process.

Testing

We then conducted tests on each prototype to gauge people's perceptions and to evaluate the effectiveness of our ideas. I wore and carried the prototypes around the university, assuming the role of a night shift worker. This allowed me to explain to people what these artifacts were and how they aided me during the night shift.

Final Outcome

Afterhours offers a glimpse into the nocturnal world of London's hotel industry, highlighting the challenges faced by night shift workers. This immersive project aims to spark conversations and drive change by presenting interventions designed to address issues such as disrupted sleep patterns and social isolation. By showcasing these interventions in an interactive setting, the project encourages dialogue and fosters empathy, ultimately advocating for better support and understanding for night shift workers.

Sleep Serum

The Sleep Serum concept addresses the sleep challenges encountered by hotel night shift workers, reflecting our research on their daytime sleep difficulties and their long term health risks. Crafted to resemble hotel shampoo bottles, the serums are presented on a dedicated display. When activated by pressing, each bottle triggers a corresponding video projection, showcasing the benefits of the specific serum.

  • Phototherapy: Balances light exposure to counteract the effects of diminished daylight and artificial blue light.
  • Noise Block: Cancels daytime noise for a quieter sleep environment.
  • Meditative Calm: Induces relaxation and reduces overstimulation, promoting anxiety-free rest.
  • Temperature Control: Regulates body temperature for optimal sleep conditions.
  • Schedule Time: Facilitates uninterrupted sleep by optimizing daytime scheduling for consistent rest.

Conversation Lamp

The Conversation Lamp highlights the isolation faced by hotel night shift workers. These workers often lack social interaction due to their unique schedules, leaving little time to connect with others. To address this, we designed a scenario where a night shift worker engages in conversation with a "talking lamp," symbolizing their need for companionship during solitary hours. This choice of a lamp integrates seamlessly with the setting, effectively conveying the message.

Hotel Key Cards

Hotel Key Cards symbolize the daily struggles of hotel night shift workers. By interacting with these workers and gathering their stories, we uncovered compelling narratives illuminating their challenges. Our goal is to raise awareness about these difficulties, especially regarding the behaviors of nighttime hotel guests. To convey these experiences, we incorporated the workers' stories onto hotel keycards, offering guests insights into the realities of night shift work.

Night-Shift Menu

The Night Shift Menu artifact sheds light on the issue of poor dietary habits among hotel night shift workers. Facing limited food options during their shifts, these workers often turn to unhealthy choices like greasy pizzas and sugary drinks, leading to serious long-term health consequences. To underscore this concern, we devised a satirical food menu, highlighting the critical need for improved nutrition among night shift workers.
