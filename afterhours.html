After Hours reimagines the nocturnal world of London's hotel industry, spotlighting the challenges faced by night shift workers. Through immersive experiences and tangible artefacts, the project seeks to raise awareness and advocate for better conditions for these essential workers. By blending narrative storytelling with critical design, After Hours invites viewers to engage with the realities of night shift work and envision a more supportive future for those working in unconventional hours.
Background

After Hours is inspired by the lives of night shift workers in the hotel industry. It seeks to shed light on their challenges and experiences, aiming to create awareness and advocate for better support and understanding for these essential workers.

Target

Our target audience encompasses the general public, policymakers, hotel management, and labor rights organizations. Through our project, we aim to raise awareness and advocate for better support for night shift workers in the hotel industry.

Research

We utilized several research methods to thoroughly understand the environment and the lives of hotel night shift workers in London.

Directed story-telling

Directed storytelling involved collecting personal stories from night shift workers in the hotel industry. These narratives shed light on the challenges they face daily, including isolation, irregular sleep patterns, and dietary issues. Through these stories, we gained valuable insights into the experiences and needs of night shift workers.

Findings

Through Directed Storytelling, we gained insights into the challenges faced by night shift workers in hotels. They highlighted concerns about their health, especially sleep patterns, alongside the positives like flexibility and pay. However, the interviews underscored the need for better support and understanding.

Love and break-up letter

In this exercise, night shift workers in the hotel industry wrote love and break-up letters to their jobs. These letters provided a unique opportunity to delve into the emotional connection and challenges experienced by workers during their night shifts. By personifying their relationship with work, we gained nuanced insights into their feelings and experiences, offering a deeper understanding of their perspective.

Findings

The Love/Breakup Letters revealed a mix of emotions from night shift workers. They appreciated financial incentives and free time but expressed concerns about health and personal life tolls. Overall, the letters emphasized the complex relationship between workers and their profession, urging solutions for improved conditions.

Psychogeography walk

The psychogeography walk involved exploring London's nocturnal landscape to understand the environment in which night shift workers operate. By immersing ourselves in the city's after-dark rhythm, we gained a deeper appreciation for the challenges and opportunities present in the nighttime economy. This experiential research method provided valuable context for our project.

Findings

Our Psychogeography walk in Leicester Square provided firsthand insights into the nighttime environment. Divided into groups, we documented our observations through audio recordings and photos. Key findings included the irregular work schedules and disrupted sleep patterns of night shift workers, along with safety concerns and the reliance on artificial light. The experience underscored the need for better-prepared interview questions to capture relevant insights effectively.

AEIOU

The AEIOU framework allowed us to analyze the activities, environments, interactions, objects, and users involved in the night shift work experience. Through this method, we identified key pain points and opportunities for improvement. By systematically examining each element, we developed a holistic understanding of the night shift worker's journey and the factors that influence their work environment.

Findings

During our AEIOU analysis, we observed various aspects of London's nightlife, shedding light on the nighttime atmosphere. Safety concerns emerged, particularly for female group members due to increased intoxication levels and a perceived lack of police presence. Additionally, observations highlighted the prevalence of soft lighting, bustling crowds, and diverse demographics.
